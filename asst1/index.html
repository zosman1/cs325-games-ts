Escape The Darkness

  • Originality: While the concept of running away from the dark is not really new, i really haven't seen anything like this before. It's similar to one of those games where you need to beat enemys for x time in order to get "extracted" to saftey. But of course, it doesn't really play in that way.
  • Technical Merit: The map is actually two maps, one overlayed on the other to create the illusion of a "light" The grabble chemicals despawns when it's "poured" into the beaker, and spawns a new one back in the drawer. There are two game loops, one to update the ui and one to update the game variables, this allows for faster and slightly more efficient rendering. All images and music and drawn myself, and composed by myself.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it? I learned that phaser is pretty nice. However, the way I had to implement the "light" was incredibly annoying and extremely frustrating to implement. I learned that I can prototype simple ideas rather quickly in phaser, and that making sure code is clean is an important thing to do from the start of the project.

  • Prototype Goal: The mechanic is a simple "dragging as fast as you can" type game. I decided to go with something mechnically simple for my first game so I could get a better idea of how phaser works.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to be slightly frustated at first, and have to figure out just how they can beat this game. On the second or third playthrough I'd like the player to be able to beat the game.
